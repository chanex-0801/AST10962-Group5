New Territories

New Territories North “Ghost Takeaway”

In 1989, a tea restaurant in Tai Po Tin received mysterious takeout calls for three consecutive days, and the cash returned turned into ghost money. Police broke down the door and found four corpses inside, with recent takeout food still inside the bodies. This is considered Hong Kong's only paranormal event "acknowledged by the government," and is shrouded in mystery.


Sai Kung “Mystical Boundary”

Hikers have mysteriously disappeared in the Sai Kung area, which is known locally as "Hong Kong's Bermuda Triangle." The most famous case is that of Ding Lihua, who disappeared in the 1990s and remains unsolved. The complex terrain and unusual magnetic fields add to the area's mystique.


Yuen Long Tat Tak School

Tat Tak School in Yuen Long is known as "Hong Kong's most haunted school" due to its abandoned buildings and its connection to World War II and colonial history, making it a central location for urban legends. It was even selected as one of the "Top Ten Scariest Places in Asia" by National Geographic Channel in 2013 and is often used in film and television works (such as TVB's "Our Unwinding Ethos") to explore urban legends.
