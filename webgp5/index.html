Urban Legends

Urban legends are a type of modern folklore, often passed down through word of mouth, which increases their credibility. They typically contain elements of fear, warning, or dark humor, and are set in everyday life scenarios such as subways, schools, shopping malls, or highways, creating a sense of intimacy and unease. A key characteristic of urban legends is their constant evolution over time and with different storytellers, oscillating between reality and fiction, reflecting the public's fear of the unknown, and embodying the collective imagination and cultural shadows of society.

Classic examples include Japan's "Slit-Mouthed Woman" and "Miss Hanako,"

Hong Kong's "The Girl with the Braids" and "The Windsor Mansion Fox Fairy Incident,"

and the United States' "Bloody Mary" and "Slender Man."
